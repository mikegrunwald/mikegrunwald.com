description: >-
  ### TL;DR


  Built an internal brand guidelines documentation site for Spotify that leaned hard into their design and motion aesthetics — treating the docs themselves as a showcase of the brand language they were describing.


  ### Impact


  * Delivered an internal brand resource that functioned as both documentation and inspiration

  * Gave Spotify teams a firsthand experience of the brand's motion and design principles in action

  * Established a reference point for how far the visual language could stretch


  ### Description


  Spotify's internal teams needed a living reference for their brand guidelines — but a static doc wasn't going to cut it. We were handed an unusual brief: push the design and motion as far as we could, and they'd pull us back if needed. They didn't pull us back much.


  As Senior Frontend Developer, I brought the visual language to life in the browser — translating Spotify's kinetic identity into scroll-driven interactions, expressive transitions, and layered motion that made the documentation feel as alive as the brand itself. The site wasn't just describing Spotify's aesthetic. It was demonstrating it.
